---
title: "Méditations Métaphysiques - René Descartes"
description: "René Descartes' foundational work in philosophy"
---

Descartes' _Méditations Métaphysiques_ is not an easy read, I will warn you from the start ! This one requires patience and a bit of philosophical backbone. Descartes takes you on a journey of radical doubt, systematically questioning everything he thinks he knows until he reaches what he considers to be the only unquestionable truth : the famous "cogito, ergo sum" (I think, therefore I am !). From there, he rebuilds his entire understanding of reality, tackling everything from the nature of mind and body to the existence of God. Fair warning though, this is not the book to read casually on the beach and you will find yourself reading the same sentences over and over again to understand them...
